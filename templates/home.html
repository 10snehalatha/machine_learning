Agriculture is the backbone of the Indian economy. Nearly 70% of the Indian population, directly or indirectly, depends on agriculture and its related activities. Rice (Oryza sativa) is the major staple food for Southern and Eastern Indians. As it is a tropical plant, the Indian climate conditions (hot and humid) are highly suitable for its cultivation which thus led India to become the second-largest producer and the largest exporter of rice grains.

The second goal of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, as adopted by the United Nations in 2015, aims for “food security”, “improved nutrition” (along with other objectives) by promoting “sustainable agriculture”.
